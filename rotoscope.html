Adobe After Effects

Adobe After Effects is a digital visual effects, motion graphics, and compositing software used for creating animations, video effects, and cinematic visual content.

  • Professional frame-by-frame animation techniques
  • Roto Brush Tool
  • Edge Refinement Controls
  • Alpha Channel & Matte View Modes
  • Creative artistic expression and style

📅 Timeline

1 to 5 seconds
▶

Open After Effects and import the video clip you want to rotoscope.

6 to 10 seconds
▶

Drag your footage into the timeline to create a composition.

11 to 15 seconds
▶

Press Ctrl + D (Windows) or Cmd + D (Mac) to duplicate the layer — this helps preserve the original.

16 to 20 seconds
▶

Click on the duplicated layer where you'll apply the rotoscoping effect.

21 to 25 seconds
▶

Double-click the duplicated layer in the timeline to open it in the Layer panel (not the Composition panel).

26 to 30 seconds
▶

Click the Roto Brush Tool from the toolbar (or press Alt/Option + W).

31 to 35 seconds
▶

Use the green brush to highlight the object you want to isolate — After Effects will detect the edges.

36 to 40 seconds
▶

Hold Alt (Windows) or Option (Mac) and draw with the red brush to subtract unwanted areas.

41 to 45 seconds
▶

Once you're happy with the selection across all frames, click the Freeze button in the Layer panel to lock in the rotoscoping.

46 to 50 seconds
▶

Click back to the Composition panel to view your isolated subject.

51 to 55 seconds
▶

Place a new background or visual effects behind your rotoscoped subject for compositing.

55 seconds to 1 minute
▶

Go to Composition > Add to Render Queue or use Media Encoder to export your final video.
